About me

I'm a PostDoc in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at University of California, Riverside, working in Fabio Pasqualetti’s group.

I’ve earned my Ph.D. (summa cum laude) in Information Engineering at the University of Padova, under the supervision of Augusto Ferrante in February 2018. During my Ph.D. I spent a visiting period (October 2015 - June 2016) in the Control Group at the University of Cambridge, under the supervision of Rodolphe Sepulchre. Prior to that, I received a M.Sc. in Automation Engineering and a B.Sc. in Mechatronics Engineering (summa cum laude) both from University of Padova.

My current research interests are in the areas of Control and Analysis of Dynamical Networks, with a special focus on biological applications. Other interests include problems of Systems Theory, Stochastic Modeling and Estimation, and Quantum Information Theory.

By the way, I love Cinematography (the masterpieces of Robert Bresson, in particular) and (pure and applied) Mathematics.
