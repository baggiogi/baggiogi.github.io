About me

I'm a fixed-term Assistant Professor (RTD-A) in the Department of Information Engineering at University of Padova, Italy.

From March 2018 to September 2019 I was a PostDoctoral Scholar in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at University of California, Riverside, advised by Fabio Pasqualetti. In February 2018, I’ve received my Ph.D. (cum laude) in the Automatic Control Group of the University of Padova, under the supervision of Augusto Ferrante. During my Ph.D. I spent a visiting period (October 2015 - June 2016) in the Control Group of the University of Cambridge, under the supervision of Rodolphe Sepulchre. Prior to that, I received a M.Sc. in Automation Engineering and a B.Sc. in Mechatronics Engineering both from the University of Padova.

[Curriculum Vitæ]

My current research interests lie broadly in the area of Control and Analysis of Dynamical Networks, with a special emphasis on biological applications. Other active interests include topics of Systems and Control Theory, Stochastic Modeling and Estimation, and Quantum Information Theory.
